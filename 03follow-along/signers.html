Who Were The Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson, at 33, was the primary author of the Declaration of Independence, drafting it in a rented Philadelphia home between June 11-28, 1776, influenced by John Locke and Thomas Paine, on a Committee of Five including Adams and Franklin, though Congress significantly edited his original draft, notably removing his condemnation of slavery. He penned the enduring words about unalienable rights, but later wished to return to Virginia, ultimately leaving his legacy to include not just the Declaration, but also Virginia's religious freedom statute and the University of Virginia, as noted on his gravestone.
John Adams
John Adams was a primary force behind the American Revolution and the Declaration of Independence in 1776, serving on the "Committee of Five" to draft it
